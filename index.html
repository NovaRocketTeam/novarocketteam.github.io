Rocket Timeline

Born in Faculdade de Ciências e Tecnologia of NOVA University of Lisbon last year with the will to learn new concepts, explore the unknown and test our limits. The project's team dedicates not just to learning how to design, assemble, and launch rockets, but also to improve its efficiency from paper electronics for weight reduction to rocket motor designs.

Currently we are in talks with Seixal municipality to get permission to launch at Aeromodelling Track of Seixal. We hope to launch rocket mid-May. The rocket will reach 300 m under simulation with electronics to measure the altitude, speed and rotation. Every part of the rocket made by us with paper, balsa, and 3d printed parts, except the motor engine.

Development of motor engine made of Potassium Nitrate/Sorbitol (KNSB), with paper electronics developed at CENIMAT|i3n and new body materials.

Still under discussion, it will be our most challenging rocket. Using previously used technologies and improving in our mistakes, it will probably be launched in Spain.

The final frontier is Space, at 100 Km.

About Us

This project was born in Faculdade de Ciências e Tecnologia of NOVA University of Lisbon last year with the will to learn new concepts, explore the unknown and test our limits. The project's team dedicates not just to learning how to design, assemble, and launch rockets, but also to improve its efficiency from paper electronics for weight reduction to rocket motor designs.

Even tought model rocketry is a very popular hobby in other countries in Portugal there is a very small culture. So we also aim to educate and inspire the next generation of engineers.
